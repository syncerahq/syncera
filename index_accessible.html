What makes this different

Not popularity. Not approval. Accuracy.

IMDb and Rotten Tomatoes measure whether people liked something. Syncera measures whether audience reality matched what people were told to expect.

Why it matters

People deserve better than vague labels and guesswork.

Film ratings influence what we watch, what kids are exposed to, and how platforms recommend content — yet many people feel those ratings do not always match reality.

This study is designed to measure that gap using real audience experiences, not assumptions.

Current study

Study SI-001: film rating perception

Our first public study measures whether official U.S. film ratings match what real audiences say they actually experience — including tone, intensity, and content mismatch.

What to expect
  • Anonymous participation
  • About 6–8 minutes to complete
  • Questions about trust, mismatch, intensity, and “vibe” accuracy
  • Optional title-specific responses to help build a live audience pulse
Why take this survey?
  • Your response contributes to real audience-based data.
  • It helps improve how content is labeled and understood.
  • It supports more accurate and accountable media systems.
